Introduction

I am a Master's student at ECE Department at UC San Diego, where I am conducting research at Advanced Robotics and Controls Lab (ARClab) under the supervision of Prof. Michael Yip. Before that, I received my Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering from UC San Diego, and doing my undergraduate research at Statistical Visual Computing Laboratory (SVCL). Starting in 2019, I have also been closely collaborating with Educational Vision Technologies (EVT) in developing the online learning system using Machine Learning techniques. My research interests lie in Computer Vision and Robotics, especially the collaboration between the two. Currently, my research focus on solving perception problems for surgical robotics using deep learning techniques.

Publications


SuPer Deep: A Surgical Perception Framework for Robotic Tissue Manipulation using Deep Learning for Feature Extraction
 Jingpei Lu*, Ambareesh Jayakumari*, Florian Richter, Yang Li, and Michael C. Yip (* Equal contributions)
arXiv, 2020.

In this work, we exploited deep learning methods for surgical perception. We integrated deep neural networks, capable of efficient feature extraction, into the tissue reconstruction and instrument pose estimation processes, which requires minimal training data and reduced feature engineering efforts to fully perceive a surgical scene.
[arXiv]


SuPer: A Surgical Perception Framework for Endoscopic Tissue Manipulation with Surgical Robotics
Yang Li*, Florian Richter*, Jingpei Lu, Emily K. Funk, Ryan K. Orosco, Jianke Zhu, and Michael C. Yip (* Equal contributions)
IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters, vol. 5, no. 2, pp. 2294-2301, April 2020.

In this work, we propose a novel surgical perception framework, SuPer, for surgical robotic control. This framework continuously collects 3D geometric information that allows for mapping a deformable surgical field while tracking rigid instruments within the field.
[arXiv] [Website]
